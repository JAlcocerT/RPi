How to read VPD

Vapor Pressure Deficit is the gap between how much water the air could hold at the current temperature and how much it actually holds. It controls plant transpiration and is a much better climate target than humidity alone.

  • 0.0–0.4 kPa · Danger. Air saturated. Mold, mildew, poor nutrient uptake.
  • 0.4–0.8 kPa · Propagation. Soft tissue, weak roots — keep humid.
  • 0.8–1.2 kPa · Vegetative. Healthy transpiration, fast growth.
  • 1.2–1.6 kPa · Flowering. Strong transpiration, dense fruit/bloom.
  • 1.6+ kPa · Stress. Stomata close, growth stalls.
Formula 
SVP(T) = 0.6108 · exp( 17.27·T / (T + 237.3) )    [kPa]
VPD    = SVP(T) · (1 − RH/100)                   [kPa]

Air-VPD approximation (assumes leaf temp ≈ air temp). For leaf-VPD subtract ~2 °C from T when computing SVP for the leaf.
